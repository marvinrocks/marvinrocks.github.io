Career Summary

Steven Gerrard spent the majority of his career at Liverpool. He made his debut on 29 November 1998 as a last minute substitution for Vegard Heggem. He made thirteen appearences in his debut season, filling in for injured captain Jamie Redknapp. In the 1999-2000 season, Gerrard got his first red card in a derby against Everton, later that season he also scored his first senior goal in a 4-1 victory against Sheffield Wednesday. In the 2000-2001 season whe scored 10 goals in all competitions, also winning the FA Cup, the League Cup, and the UEFA Cup. In the next season, he went on to win both the FA Charity Shield and the UEFA Super Cup. Gerrard replaced Sami Hyypia as the captain of Liverpool. In the second half of the Champions League final against AC Milan, Liverpool came back from a three goal defecit to tie the match 3-3. Liverpool won on penalties, with Gerrard named the Man of the Match and the UEFA Club Footballer of the Year Award. In the 2005-2006 season, Gerrard scored twice against West Ham in the 2006 FA Cup Final, becoming the only player to have scored in the FA Cup, League Cup, UEFA Cup and Champions League finals. He made his 100th appearance in European club competition for Liverpool on 10 March 2009 against Real Madrid and scored twice in a 4–0 win. Four days after the impressive victory over Real, Gerrard would score at Old Trafford for the first time from the penalty spot, putting Liverpool ahead on their way to a 4–1 victory over Manchester United. Following these results, three-time FIFA World Player of the Year Zinedine Zidane hailed the Liverpool skipper as being the best player in the world. On 22 March 2009, Gerrard scored his first ever hat-trick in the Premier League, against Aston Villa, in a 5–0 victory. In 2015, Gerrard signed for LA Galaxy, where he currently plays.

Achievements

Gerrard won the FA Cup twice with Liverpool, once in 2001 and once in 2006. He has won the Football League Cup in 2001, 2003, and 2012. He has also won the FA Community Shield in 2006,the UEFA Champions League in 2005, the UEFA Cup in 2001 and the UEFA Super Cup in 2001 and 2005.
